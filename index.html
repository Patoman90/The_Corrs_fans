The Corrs

Introducing the Corrs

The Corrs are and Irish rock pop band who combine traditional Irish instruments, rock and vocals together to create a unique flavour of music they are known for. The band is made up of siblings Sharon, Caroline, Andrea and Jim. They set up the band in the 1990's and released alot of their own music as well as covers of other songs. The band is from Dundalk county Louth in Ireland. The band has been active since 1991 and took a break in 2006. After they had established their own families they reunited in 2016 and have been active since. They have a broad fanbase in Ireland and Europe and the rest of the world. They have played at a variety of venues and events including the Special Olymics. The band members themselves too have taken the time to help out with a variety of charities.

Meet the Corrs

Andrea Jane Corr

Andrea Corr is the lead singer of the band. She is the youngest member and sister born on 17th May 1974. She does lead vocals and plays the tin whistle in some of their music. She has a very natural look about her and has always kept herself modest while being able to rock her beauty. She is married and has kids of her own. She has taken part in charity work and did some acting and solo songs too. She has been given a honorary MBE for her contributions to music and charity.

Jim Corr

James Steven Ignatius Corr better known as Jim Corr, is the eldest sibling and guitarist with the band. Jim joins in with vocals with his siblings when they all sing parts of their songs collectively. He is the plays guitar but also plays keyboard, piano, mandolin and accordion. Jim was born on July 31st 1964. He has taken part in charity events and work too. Jim views himself as a truth seeker and has spoken openly about his views on various interviews. Unfortunately Jim has had his share of critism for it. He is a song writer and also has worked with productions outside of the band. He too has a MBE award for his charitable work.

Sharon Corr

Sharon Helga Corr is the back up vocals for the band and often plays her Violin to add a majestic vibe to their music. She plays piano and guitar too. She started learning violin at 6 years old. She was born on the 24th of March 1970 and has had a solo career where she is the lead singer and has showcased her violin and piano skills. She has had television appearances too which includes The voice of Ireland in 2012. She too has a MBE award for her part in charitable work.

Caroline Corr

Caroline Georgina Corr also know as the "Chick who's got stick" is the drummer for the band. Often hidden behind the drums in the background she joins in with vocals. She is a talented drummer and plays traditional instruments like the bodhran, cajon, percussions and piano. One of the middle children and second youngest, she was born 17th March 1973. She was born on St Patrick's day. She took part in Women in Harmony which involved Irish female singers and musicians that recorded dreams in aid of Safe Ireland in 2020. She also got a MBE for her part in charity work.
